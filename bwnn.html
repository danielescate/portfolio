Best Worst News Network (BWNN)

An interactive video that is controlled by the viewer.

In this project, the viewer will direct the flow of the news broadcast and how well, or poorly, it is executed. News anchors usually have assistance from other people through teleprompters or in-ear monitors.
In this scenario, the viewer is that person on the other side. They control what to cut to next (ex. news story to weather) and how well that portion goes (ex. sending wrong information to the news anchor. The view plays a crucial part in the interactive video. While waiting for the viewer's response, the anchor will appear to be clueless, not knowing what to do.
Adding to this, if the viewer decides to send false information, the anchor will be caught off guard, again not knowing what to do, but wanting to be professional and progress the news broadcast since they are “LIVE”. It’s entirely up to the viewer if they want to see a somewhat good or very bad news broadcast
